## Overview

Milestone `v1.8` made unreliable-network, large-file, browser-origin uploads a
shipped Rindle capability. It added a mountable bare `Rindle.Upload.TusPlug`,
Local and S3-backed tus resumable ingest that still converges into the existing
`verify_completion/2` promote lane, browser→Mux direct creator upload, and the
final generated-app proof and traceability closure needed to ship the whole
browser-ingest story as one coherent contract.

## Key Accomplishments

- Shipped a bare `Plug` tus 1.0 edge with HMAC-signed URLs, strict HEAD/PATCH /
  POST / DELETE semantics, and no Phoenix dependency.
- Added the `:tus_upload` capability plus `resumable_protocol` discriminator so
  v1.7's resumable substrate now supports both provider-direct and
  server-mediated browser ingest honestly.
- Landed Local tmp-append and S3 multipart-per-PATCH tus backing, including
  completion through the unchanged `verify_completion/2` lane.
- Closed the hard failure cases around abort recovery, cross-node resume
  integrity, tail cleanup, and reaper behavior, with live MinIO proof.
- Shipped adopter-facing auth/error/telemetry/doctor/docs support for tus and
  restored the generated-app tus install-smoke proof with fresh evidence.
- Completed the deferred v1.6/v1.7 sibling work for browser→Mux direct creator
  upload, including webhook correlation, public entrypoints, LiveView support,
  and end-to-end proof.

### Milestone Goal

Make unreliable-network, large-file, browser-origin uploads durable: ship the
tus 1.0 protocol on a mountable bare Plug, S3 multipart and Local backing,
browser→Mux direct creator upload, and keep every browser ingest path converged
on the one trusted `verify_completion/2` promote lane.
