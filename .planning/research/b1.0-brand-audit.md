# b1.0 Brand Audit — Pressure Test of `prompts/rindle-brand-book.md`

**Date:** 2026-06-10 · **Requirement:** BRAND-01, BRAND-02 · **Verdict framework:** KEEP / TIGHTEN / REWORK / ADD / REMOVE
**Method:** ten-lens critical review + measured WCAG 2.1 contrast verification (no claim accepted without a number).

---

## 1. Executive judgment

**The seed is strong enough to build from.** This is not a generic AI brand book: it has a
real conceptual center ("a protected current" — media carried through a safe path), a
specific anti-trope posture (no Elixir purple, no cloud-upload arrows, no AI sparkles, no
SaaS hype), and — unusually — its voice/microcopy sections are its *best* material, which
is exactly backwards from most brand documents and exactly right for an OSS library whose
primary surfaces are READMEs, docs, and error messages.

Direct answers:

- **Strong enough to build from?** Yes. ~70% ports forward with light tightening.
- **Distinct enough?** Yes, conceptually. Green-teal + warm shell + "calm operations
  console" is unclaimed territory in the Elixir/devtools space. The *visual* identity is
  only directional (three logo sketches, no mark) — that's Phase 82's job, not a defect.
- **Implementation-ready?** Partially. Colors and fonts are tokenizable today; but there
  is no dark-mode system, no focus spec that survives measurement, no spacing scale, and
  type sizes are ranges ("56–72px") rather than tokens.
- **Over- or under-specified?** Balanced overall; under-specified exactly where
  implementation starts (tokens, states, dark mode) and slightly over-specified in
  generation artifacts (SAML word lists, LLM prompts) that don't belong in a published book.
- **Highest-leverage improvement:** a complete dark-mode token set. Most developers will
  meet Rindle on dark GitHub and dark HexDocs; the seed designs almost entirely for light.
- **Do not change:** the brand idea, the five pillars, the personality table, the voice
  formula, and the entire microcopy bank (§12–14). The palette family and type trio also
  stay — measured, they hold up.

**One decisive factual finding:** the "existing logo" at
`examples/adoption_demo/priv/static/images/logo.svg` is the **Phoenix Framework firebird**
(Phoenix's own path data, Phoenix's trademark orange `#FD4F00`) — a borrowed placeholder,
not a Rindle design direction. There is no orange-vs-green decision to make: shipping a
Rindle identity in Phoenix's mark/color would read as impersonation of the framework it
integrates with. The seed's green-teal palette stands unopposed. *(Recorded as decision
D-b1.0-01 below.)*

## 8. Direction-lock brief (BRAND-02)

Everything below is **locked** for phases 82–85. Each line is marked *confirmed* (from
seed, survives audit) or *revised* (with the audit-lens failure that justifies it).

### Palette — confirmed, with three additions
- All core + semantic hexes from seed §6 stand unchanged (*confirmed by measurement*).
- ADD `--rindle-border-strong` (target ≥3:1 vs Porcelain; tuned in Phase 84) — *revised:
  accessibility lens, measured 1.4.11 failure*.
- ADD light-mode focus = Deep Current; dark-mode focus = Rindle Green — *revised:
  accessibility lens, measured 1.81 vs 3:1*.
- ADD full dark-mode set in Phase 84 (surfaces from Ink/Code BG family; text from
  Porcelain/Code Text family; semantic colors re-tuned for dark) — *revised: durability
  lens, dark-first developer surfaces*.
- Rule encoded in tokens: Rindle Green and Rind Lime are accent/large-graphic-only on
  light surfaces; text-capable only on Ink/dark — *confirmed (seed prose → token metadata)*.

### Typography — confirmed
- Space Grotesk (display) / Atkinson Hyperlegible (body) / JetBrains Mono (code). OFL.
  Committed: woff2 for the brand book, ttf for SVG generation, license files alongside.
- Ranges become fixed tokens in Phase 84 — *revised: design-token lens*.

### Logo — direction guidance for Phase 82 (locked constraints)
- Lowercase `rindle` wordmark; prose stays "Rindle" — *confirmed*.
- NO container/background shape of any kind behind marks; boundary-breaking encouraged —
  *user constraint*.
- Logotype optically tight to mark (~0.4× cap-height gap, tuned optically) — *user constraint*.
- Main lockup carries NO subtitle; a single `-subtitle` variant carries "Media, made durable." — *user constraint*.
- ≥2 of 5 candidates are integrated custom typemarks (motif worked into letterforms) — *user constraint*.
- Seed Route B (container mark) is dead; Routes A (channel) and C (recipe branch) feed
  candidates — *revised: violates container constraint*.
- Seed logo don'ts (no upload arrows, shutters, padlocks, cylinders, purple potions,
  sparkles) remain binding — *confirmed*.

### Voice — confirmed
- Voice formula, microcopy bank, copy bank, do/don'ts port verbatim into the HTML book.
- SAML word-list collapsed to one sentence; §18 prompts and the legal-amber note do not
  enter the book — *revised: maintainability lens (generation residue)*.

### Decisions recorded

- **D-b1.0-01 — Placeholder logo:** `examples/adoption_demo/priv/static/images/logo.svg`
  is the Phoenix Framework firebird (`#FD4F00`, Phoenix's path data), a borrowed
  placeholder. It is **not** a Rindle design direction and must be replaced by the Phase 83
  mark in a follow-up (demo re-theming itself is out of b1.0 scope). The seed's green-teal
  palette is adopted unopposed. Shipping Phoenix's mark/color as Rindle identity would
  constitute framework impersonation.
- **D-b1.0-02 — No seed rewrite:** `prompts/rindle-brand-book.md` stays untouched as the
  historical seed. The HTML brand book (`brandbook/index.html`, Phase 84) is the canonical
  successor.
- **D-b1.0-03 — Name risk = human review only:** "rindle" collides with an npm streams
  utility and older PM products (seed header). The package already ships on hex.pm as
  `rindle`. Trademark/legal clearance is flagged for **human review** and is explicitly
  out of b1.0 scope. No renaming, no legal work, no brand copy about it.
